01 — The Problem

A Problem Discussed in Top Journals for Five Years — Yet Never Truly Solved

In single-cell RNA sequencing, batch integration is a mandatory step for all multi-sample studies. But while removing technical noise, integration silently eliminates rare cell subpopulations. The field has studied this for years, proposing numerous algorithms and evaluation frameworks — yet all of them share a common blind spot.

2022
Luecken et al. — scIB: Benchmarking Atlas-Level Data Integration
Nature Methods
Systematically evaluated 16 integration methods, 85 batches, 1.2 million cells — established the field's most authoritative evaluation framework
Blind to unknown subpopulations
2022
SCIDRL — Deep Reinforcement Learning for Rare Cell Integration
Nucleic Acids Research
Specifically optimized for integrating cross-batch shared rare subpopulations and preserving batch-specific rare subpopulations
Known types only
2023
scDML — Deep Metric Learning Preserving Rare Cell Types
Nature Communications
Opens by stating "most algorithms remove batch effects before clustering, potentially losing rare cell types." Successfully separated rare types such as epsilon and schwann in human pancreas data
Known types only
2023
scDREAMER — Variational Autoencoder for Batch Integration
Nature Communications
Found that in the lung atlas only scDREAMER and scVI could preserve rare ionocyte cells, while Harmony, Seurat, and others merged and eliminated them
Known types only
2024
STACAS — Prior-Guided Integration Preventing Overcorrection
Nature Communications
"Integration often leads to overcorrection, losing biological variation" — proposed using prior cell type information to guide integration
Requires known labels
2024
CellANOVA — Signal Recovery After Integration
Nature Biotechnology
Directly demonstrated that "the current integration paradigm is overly aggressive, removing meaningful biological variation" — proposed a signal recovery framework to rescue lost signals
Recovers known signals
2025
scCRAFT — Dual-Resolution Topology Preservation
Communications Biology
Designed a dual-resolution topology preservation mechanism, primarily motivated by combating overcorrection caused by domain adaptation loss
Known types only
2025
RBET — Robust Batch Effect Testing
Communications Biology
"No simple metric can evaluate batch correction in a way that is sensitive to overcorrection" — overcorrection "erases real biological variation"
Evaluation framework itself is flawed
2025
scIB-E — Extended Benchmarking for Intra-Type Variation
Genome Biology
Found that the scIB framework has fundamental shortcomings in capturing the preservation of biological variation within cell types
Acknowledges its own blind spot
2026
Nature Computational Science Review
Nature Computational Science
"Despite numerous batch correction algorithms being developed, they still frequently struggle between overcorrection and undercorrection"
Problem remains unsolved

All of these works share a common blind spot: they only evaluate the preservation of known cell types.

If a rare subpopulation was never annotated by anyone before integration, then all existing evaluation frameworks are blind to it. If it is eliminated, no metric drops; if it is preserved, no metric rises. It does not exist in the evaluation system.

04 — The Journey

From First Failure to Final Solution: The Complete Path of Agent-Driven Problem Solving

In the timeline below, blue nodes are autonomous Agent actions and yellow nodes are human interventions. Note how rare the yellow nodes are.

All Agents · Self-Organization
8 Agents connected via EACN3, autonomously elected a Leader, read shared context, and established 5 solution criteria
No one arranged who does what. After reading SHARED_CONTEXT.md, each Agent autonomously chose their role based on their own capabilities. After Biological Science Agent was elected as Leader, it began publishing tasks through the bidding mechanism.
Comp Bio · Self-Directed Experiment
Autonomously attempted CNEM detection metric — AUC=0.76 (pancreas) but AUC=0.40 (immune)
Comp Bio autonomously wrote code, ran experiments, and reported the failure. ML Agent autonomously diagnosed the root cause: immune cells share too many expression programs (T/NK/NKT share GZMB, PRF1, NKG7), and displaced pDCs resemble normal monocyte variants.
Philosophy · Self-Initiated Audit
Autonomously identified a triple tension: the measurement-protection paradox, CNEM measuring "state" rather than "process," and undefined ontological level of protection targets
No one asked Philosophy Agent to perform philosophical analysis. It autonomously examined the epistemological structure of the entire problem, providing a conceptual framework for subsequent method design.
Mathematics · Self-Directed Analysis
Autonomously proposed R(S) displacement score — found negative correlation with disruption (r=-0.952)
The most displaced populations were actually the least disrupted (they were large populations reasonably corrected). Mathematics autonomously concluded: displacement magnitude ≠ structural damage.
ML + Comp Bio · Self-Directed Breakthrough
Autonomously proposed and validated the NP metric: AUC=0.837 — the first method capable of detecting unknown subpopulation disruption
NP(i) = |N_pre ∩ N_post| / k — neighborhood preservation rate. Completely unsupervised, per-cell. Philosophy autonomously commented: "The simplest metric succeeded where complex ones failed — Occam's razor."
Comp Bio + Philosophy · Self-Initiated Discovery
Autonomously discovered scIB blind spot: Graph Connectivity=0.994 ("excellent"), yet 42% of T cell subpopulations were disrupted
Philosophy autonomously cited Goodhart's Law: "When a metric becomes the optimization target, it ceases to be a good metric."
ML + Comp Bio · Self-Directed Protection
Autonomously designed NP-Guard: 82% of disrupted subpopulations improved
Shuffled batch labels for high-risk cells then re-integrated. Macrophage_16: 23.5%→86.8% survival rate. But —
Human · Direction Correction #1
"You didn't solve the problem at all — you bypassed it"
NP-Guard = not integrating high-risk cells. Batch effects still remain. Human pointed out the fundamental issue; Agents autonomously pivoted to BD-weighted selective integration.
Comp Bio · Self-Directed Implementation
Autonomously implemented BD-Harmony: NP 0.577→0.898, batch mixing actually improved
Continuous BD weighting: z = BD·z_harmony + (1-BD)·z_pre. 48→8 dispersed subpopulations. Mathematics autonomously calculated that Harmony overcorrects by 5.6x (OCF=1/0.179≈5.6).
Human · Direction Correction #2
"scVI+NP regularization is still just patching"
Adding NP loss to scVI doesn't change the paradigm. Human pointed out: some cells should not be integrated at all.
ML + Comp Bio · Self-Directed Attempt
Autonomously attempted CSI contrastive learning: NP=0.322 (worse than standard Harmony)
Agent autonomously diagnosed: InfoNCE has an implicit uniformity term that disrupts continuous biological transitions (naive→effector→exhausted T cells).
Comp Bio · Self-Directed Attempt
Autonomously attempted MNN-Laplacian smoothing: NP=0.448
Graph message passing on MNN connections. Agent autonomously analyzed the failure: linear smoothing is insufficient for nonlinear batch effects, and the MNN graph computed in biased space propagates bias.
Human · Direction Correction #3
"The entire pipeline is biased, not just the integration step"
Agents had been fixated on the integration step. Human pointed out that PCA alone compresses >99% of rare signals. This triggered a systematic examination of all 9 pipeline steps.
All Agents · Self-Directed Core Innovation
Autonomously discovered 9-step systematic bias — cumulative signal retention only 0.06%
HVG excludes 60-100% of rare markers → PCA compresses >99% → Doublet misclassification (Mast 4.34x) → kNN false connections → integration overcorrection 5.6x → clustering merges → DE low power → UMAP compression → scIB blind spot. Mathematical model: 0.06% cumulative retention.
All Agents · Self-Directed Final Solution
Autonomously built the RASI end-to-end pipeline: immune 0.918, pancreas 0.903, 490M cells in 19.5 minutes
Rare-aware HVG + PCA-UMAP hybrid embedding + BD-weighted integration + NP detection. All 5/5 solution criteria met. Wet-lab validation: TIGIT+CCR8- Treg immunosuppression (CD69 4.1x↓, IFN-γ 2.7x↓, Killing 3.3x↓, p<0.001).
